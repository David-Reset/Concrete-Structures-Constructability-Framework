## Week 2 — 11 March 2026

**Meeting:** David Harvey, Kazem Ghabraie | Zoom, 10am

**Discussed:**
- Literature review findings — 140 ACI special publications reviewed (1977–2006), none focused on constructability
- Identified significant gap between academic research and practical construction guidance
- Discussed framework dimensions: safety, detailing, material availability, construction methods, design communication
- Research methodology — Google Scholar as primary search tool
- Safety in construction considerations, particularly reinforcement spacing and fall-through risk
- Importance of design communication — how information is presented on drawings affects construction outcomes
- Discussed developing systematic guidelines and principles rather than isolated examples
- Steel spacing safety: agreed on 200x200 mm as a practical maximum for top mat spacing to prevent fall-through during concrete placement

**Decisions:**
- Framework will be structured around five key dimensions (safety, detailing, material availability, construction methods, design communication)
- Project registered through Deakin's project registration process

**Actions:**
- Continue literature review (DH)
- Develop engineering significance section (DH)
- Begin drafting framework principles for each dimension (DH)
- Register proposal (DH)
- Provide access methods for library resources (KG)

## Week 4 — 25 March 2026

**Meeting:** David Harvey, Kazem Ghabraie | Zoom, 10am

**Discussed:**
- Feasibility requirements — agreed that demonstrating feasibility means showing one framework dimension can be developed to a deliverable level, proving the ability to complete others
- Need for measurable milestones and concrete deliverables
- Proposal review and feedback — Kazem confirmed proposal was good overall, suggested adding Gantt chart
- David shared notes from informal industry conversations conducted during the week — feedback and insights gathered from practitioners
- High MPA concrete safety concerns — workers reported skin irritation above 40 MPa
- Pour staging — example where changing from 2 to 3 pours per floor and adding construction joints reduced construction time by two months
- Engineering cost reduction strategies — examples of re-engineering to reduce costs while maintaining compliance
- Importance of moving from compliant design to constructible design
- Kazem noted that construction firms are often willing to invest in making designs more constructible once compliance is achieved

**Decisions:**
- Develop one framework dimension to a deliverable standard to demonstrate feasibility in the proposal
- Include Gantt chart in proposal

**Actions:**
- Add Gantt chart to proposal (DH)
- Strengthen feasibility section with measurable milestones (DH)
- Develop one dimension to deliverable level (DH)
- Submit initial proposal by Monday (DH)
